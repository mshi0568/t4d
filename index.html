FRONTAL LOBE

The frontal lobe helps us make decisions, make plans and solve problems. It tells us the right or wrong way to behave; for example, that we shouldn’t be rude to other people. It helps us talk, and to initiate or start things.

HEALTHY BRAIN: BRAIN WITH DEMENTIA:
  • “Sometimes my Aunty really smells, but I would never say anything because it would upset mum.”.
  • “I definitely know what I would want for dinner if asked – PIZZA - with extra cheese!”.
  • “If someone smells, I might tell them so, straight out, without thinking.”
  • "I definitely have a favourite food, but if someone asks me what I want to eat, I say I don’t know."

Parietal Lobe

The parietal lobe helps us put actions in the right order, such as knowing to put socks on before our shoes. It also helps us to know where our body is in space and how close we are to things. This helps us to walk around things in our path, to find our way, and to pick things up.

HEALTHY BRAIN: BRAIN WITH DEMENTIA:
  • “I put on my undies before my jeans”.
  • “I know my way to and from school”.
  • “I know how to get dressed, but I sometimes put on my undies after I put on my jeans, or I might not put on my undies at all!”.
  • “I get lost on my way to the shops now, even though I have been taking the same route for years”.

Temporal Lobe

The temporal lobe is where we make and keep our memories – it is how we remember things. It also helps us to understand what people are saying or understand written information.

HEALTHY BRAIN: BRAIN WITH DEMENTIA:
  • “I can remember in lots of detail what I did today and yesterday”.
  • “I understand what my friends are saying”.
  • “I have real trouble remembering what I did five minutes ago”.
  • “I sometimes find it hard to understand what people are saying, like yesterday I didn’t know what “TV” meant”.

Occipital Lobe

The occipital lobe is responsible for making sense of the information that comes in from our eyes. For example, seeing the colours in a rainbow, or helping us to recognise someone’s face.

HEALTHY BRAIN: BRAIN WITH DEMENTIA:
  • “I can tell my mum and sister apart in photos”.
  • “Sometimes it is difficult to tell my mum and sister apart in photos.”.

Cerebellum

The cerebellum looks after things that our bodies do automatically or without thinking, such as walking. It is responsible for balance and movement.

HEALTHY BRAIN: BRAIN WITH DEMENTIA:
  • “I can balance on one foot, even with my eyes closed!”.
  • “I feel wobbly when I walk and feel like I am going to fall.”.

You use your frontal lobe nearly everyday. You use it to make decisions, such as what to eat or drink for breakfast in the morning, as well as for thinking or studying for a test. The frontal lobe is also where our personality is formed and where we can carry out higher mental processes such as planning. In addition, the frontal lobe is necessary to being able to speak fluently (without fault) and meaningfully.
